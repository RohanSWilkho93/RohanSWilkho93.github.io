Digital Twin for real-time predictive flood inundation mapping for the city of Galveston, Texas

In the critical field of flood management, traditional inundation mapping has been reactive, focusing on post-disaster scenarios or simulations of hypothetical floods. Our project marks a paradigm shift, embracing the power of predictive inundation mapping. This innovative approach is not just about mapping; it's a proactive strategy to revolutionize how we prepare for floods. By accurately forecasting flood extents, we can optimize resource allocation, prepare first responders, facilitate timely evacuations, and implement preventive measures to significantly reduce potential damage.

At the core of our breakthrough is the development of a digital twin for the city of Galveston, Texas. This advanced model seamlessly integrates dynamic factors such as predicted precipitation, wind intensity, and soil moisture with static variables including land use, the storm sewer network, and proximity to water outlets. The result is a series of highly accurate, timely inundation maps that provide critical insights into future flood scenarios. These maps are a game-changer, offering the city an unprecedented ability to anticipate and respond to flooding events, ensuring greater safety and resilience for its communities.

Platform for Resilience Inference Measurement and Enhancement

Our project introduces a groundbreaking platform, harnessing machine learning to deliver an empirically validated index for measuring community resilience against disasters. This tool uniquely integrates socio-economic factors, overcoming the subjective biases and lack of empirical support seen in traditional indices.

Hosted on CyberGISx, our platform offers robust, data-driven resilience assessment. Users can select regions, and the platform computes and validates indices, revealing key socio-economic drivers. This enables precise risk assessment and informs resilience strategies, setting new standards in disaster preparedness and planning. Our approach empowers communities to respond more effectively to disasters, marking a significant stride in building a resilient future.

GIS Tool for Dynamic Assessment of Community Flash Flood Susceptibility

In the US, flash floods are notorious as the leading cause of fatalities from natural disasters, striking unexpectedly and with localized devastation. Our research addresses the unique challenge these floods present due to their rapid and regional-specific nature. We're spearheading the development of a GIS-based tool, integrating advanced machine learning, to dissect and understand the diverse factors that incite flash floods in various regions.

This innovative tool goes beyond mere data analysis; it offers a tailored assessment of community susceptibility to flash floods, considering the distinct characteristics of each region. By providing customized, region-specific insights, our approach revolutionizes flash flood prediction and community preparedness. It's not just a technological breakthrough but a crucial step towards empowering communities, enhancing their resilience, and significantly reducing the risks associated with these unpredictable natural disasters.

Watershed Flood Modeling using Machine Learning

In our innovative project, we're breaking new ground in watershed flood modeling by utilizing diverse machine learning techniques. Our goal is ambitious yet clear: to develop accurate models capable of predicting flood susceptibility in watersheds. This venture is not just about technological advancement; it's a strategic endeavor to understand and foresee flood dynamics more effectively than ever before.

The core of our breakthrough lies in creating detailed susceptibility maps. These maps are crucial for identifying high-risk areas within watersheds, enabling timely and targeted flood mitigation efforts. By providing these predictive insights, our work is instrumental in guiding real-time, preventive actions during flood events, thereby enhancing community resilience and safety against the devastating impacts of floods.
