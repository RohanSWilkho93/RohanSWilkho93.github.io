Bio

I am a passionate advocate for climate resilience, currently enriching my knowledge and skills as an interdisciplinary Ph.D. candidate in Civil Engineering at Texas A&M University, under the advisement of Dr. Nasir G. Gharaibeh. Complementing my doctoral studies, I'm also pursuing a Master's in Computer Science and a certificate in Geographic Information Sciences. My journey is fueled by a deep commitment to understanding and combating climate change to safeguard our communities for a resilient future.

My research is at the intersection of climate science, engineering and artificial intelligence, dedicated to developing innovative, data-driven methods for predicting climate change impacts. This involves an exciting blend of climate science, artificial intelligence, and geospatial analysis, all aimed at empowering communities and authorities with the tools to devise robust policies and strategies for protecting lives and properties.

Beyond the lab, I am a lead teaching assistant for the undergraduate surveying course, CVEN 303, and actively engage in various student and professional organizations. My all-round contributions have been acknowledged with prestigious recognitions like the ASFPM Student Paper Prize, Montgomery Award, and Richard Lietz Memorial Scholarship.

As I continue to explore and contribute to this vital field, my goal is not only to advance our scientific understanding but also to inspire and facilitate informed climate action on a broader scale.
