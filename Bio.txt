Rohan Singh Wilkho is a research scientist with a PhD in Civil Engineering and a Master's in Computer Science from Texas A&M University. His work focuses on integrating climate science with data-driven engineering and artificial intelligence to enhance disaster management systems and improve community resilience. Rohan develops innovative solutions ranging from AI tools for web data extraction to digital twins and metrics for disaster resilience. His research aims to support effective decision-making in climate action and infrastructure management. Starting Spring 2025, Rohan will serve as a Visiting Lecturer in Civil and Environmental Engineering at Texas A&M University. His contributions to the field have been recognized through several awards, including the ASFPM Student Paper Prize, the Montgomery Award, and the Richard Lietz Memorial Scholarship, reflecting his commitment to research excellence and community support.
